## Context

v0.1.3 shipped the distributional intrinsic-value band but held back the earnings-quality signal pending a separate gate. That gate is now passed. v0.1.4 also closes the cross-name calibration gap acknowledged at v0.1.3 ship and moves the platform's source-of-truth from local files into the data warehouse.

## Outcomes

**Earnings-quality is displayed as a letter-grade band.** Composite across cash-conversion, accruals, manipulation flags, balance-sheet stress, and intangible bloat. The band shows a high-to-low range across components rather than a single integer, and a click-to-expand drill-down exposes per-component scoring for users who want the detail.

**Historical-fraud forensic fixture clears its gate.** Five fraud-archetype filings (revenue recognition, channel stuffing, fabricated revenue, debt-funded goodwill bloat, reserve under-recognition) are classified as quality-stressed by the composite. Three established-conservative filings are classified as clean. Eight of eight fixtures land in their expected band. The fixture set is intended to remain stable across subsequent iterations of the composite.

**Cross-name calibration evidence broadened from one to five.** The spread-calibration gate now has empirical support across large-cap technology, megabank, and supermajor energy names. All five fall inside the accepted calibration band. The single-name limitation acknowledged at v0.1.3 ship is closed.

**Warehouse-first storage.** Both backtest probe results and historical filing fixtures now live in the warehouse with a full bi-temporal contract (effective date, availability timestamp, supersession discipline, content hash, vendor provenance). Local artifacts remain as release records only. Re-running the gates is a query against canonical clean views, not a file scan.

**Diagnostic granularity on gate failure.** When the calibration gate fails it now emits a named reason (null-realized-spread, zero-or-negative realized spread, ratio below band, ratio above band) rather than a binary pass-fail. Downstream automated gates can act on the distinction between a data-quality miss and a real calibration miss.

**Methodology disclosure carried forward.** Author-supplied inputs are preserved per-row in the warehouse so post-hoc review can verify the gate was not gamed by widening input ranges. Replacement of author-supplied inputs with research-stage outputs is queued.

Replacement of author-supplied calibration inputs with research-stage outputs, regime-conditioned widening of input ranges on major macro events, and dynamic position-sizing context (relative-strength, volatility regime, drop velocity) all ship in the next release. Multi-regime centering validation requires point-in-time fundamentals over a decade-plus horizon and is queued for a later release.
